Understanding COPD Through Spectrogram Analysis

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) is a progressive lung disease that causes breathing difficulties due to obstructed airflow. It is commonly associated with chronic bronchitis and emphysema, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, shortness of breath, and persistent coughing. Early detection of COPD is crucial for effective management, and one way to analyze respiratory health is through spectrogramsâ€”visual representations of sound frequencies over time.

COPD Spectrogram Analysis

The spectrogram on the right represents a breathing sound from a COPD patient. It shows irregular spikes and high-energy regions, which indicate turbulent airflow caused by airway obstruction. The presence of wheezing and crackles common in COPD appears as erratic patterns in lower and mid-range frequencies. These abnormal breathing sounds help distinguish COPD from normal respiratory function.

Healthy Breathing Spectrogram

In contrast, the healthy breathing spectrogram exhibits a smooth and structured frequency distribution, with no significant interruptions. The breathing cycles appear consistent, reflecting normal lung function with unobstructed airflow. The absence of sharp irregularities in the frequency spectrum suggests a steady and controlled respiration pattern, which is typically seen in individuals without respiratory diseases.

By comparing these spectrograms, we can observe clear differences in breathing patterns between a COPD patient and a healthy individual. Machine Learning models, particularly Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), can leverage these patterns to classify and detect COPD at an early stage, assisting in medical diagnostics and research.
